## Discussion

The like-for-like question against `data-scaling-law` r7: does adding the ignore
class as a third output degrade L/R choice prediction, and does the choice metric
still scale with D? Scoring the conditional L/R likelihood on **engaged trials**
(chance 1/2, matching the 2-way support) isolates this from the ignore-class
detection question (r2).

The answer to both: the 3-way head is free on choice, and the D-scaling story is
the same capacity-dependent shape as the 2-way study — small H saturates, large H
keeps climbing. See the metric caveat in the [study README](../../README.md): the
raw 3-way normalized likelihood (chance 1/3) is **not** comparable to the 2-way
number by subtraction, which is why this report scores the engaged-trial
conditional instead.
